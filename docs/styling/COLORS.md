## 7. Duplicate-color "5+ same color" consolidation targets

These are the prime consolidation candidates — single hardcoded RGB tuples appearing 5+ times across the tree:

1. **`rgb(255, 191, 0)` — 62 sites across 6 files.** "Amber/warning/RTH/placed-order/pending/fib." Define `t.warning` (or `t.gold_strong`) and replace all sites. **Highest ROI of any change.**
2. **`rgb(74, 158, 255)` — 28 sites in `gpu.rs`.** "Selection-handle blue." Define `t.selection` / reuse `t.accent_alt`. Almost always paired with `circle_filled` for drawing handles → trivial mechanical replace.
3. **`rgb(255, 193, 37)` — 17 sites across 4 files.** "Gold for pre-market / earnings / fib." Consolidate with #1 into a single warning gold (the two oranges are nearly indistinguishable on screen).
4. **`rgb(224, 85, 96)` — 17 sites across 4 files.** "Cancel/Delete/Danger button." Define `t.danger` and use everywhere a destructive action is colored.
5. **`rgb(46, 204, 113)` + `rgb(56, 203, 137)` + `rgb(80, 200, 120)` — 33 combined sites.** All three are "bull green." They should all become `t.bull`. The fact that the codebase has three different greens for the same semantic concept is a clear theme leak.

Honorable mentions: `rgb(231, 76, 60)` (8) + `rgb(224, 82, 82)` (8) + `rgb(230, 70, 70)` (12) = three "bear/error red" variants totalling **28** sites that should collapse onto `t.bear` / `t.error`.

`Color32::from_white_alpha(120/140/170/180)` — 8 sites (`hotkey_editor`, `object_tree`, `settings_panel`) — these break on light theme; replace with `t.dim` / `t.text` per case.
